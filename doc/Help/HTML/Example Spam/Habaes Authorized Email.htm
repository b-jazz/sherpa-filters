Habaes Authorized Email

This is actually an example of an email that you might want to consider as a normal piece of mail. There is a company out there with an interesting way to fight spam. Their goal isn't to tell you what a spam message is, but it is to tell you when a message isn't a spam. You can read all about it at the Habeas website.

The way to filter out a message that is marked with the Habeas trademark is to look for the haiku in the headers. But you really only need to look for the one header with the (tm) symbol in it. The example below shows you the filter to use.

Habeas Example

<Filter Name="IN: Habaes Authorized Email" Enabled="true" Tag="Normal">
  <Criteria>
    <HeaderMatch>
      <Header>X-Habeas-SWE-3</Header>
      <Value>^like Habeas SWE (tm)$</Value>
    </HeaderMatch>
  </Criteria>
  <Actions>
    <Move>
      <Destination>Mailboxes/In</Destination>
    </Move>
  </Actions>
  <Stats>
    <TestCounter>0</TestCounter>
    <MatchCounter>0</MatchCounter>
    <FilterTime>0</FilterTime>
    <LastMatchTime></LastMatchTime>
  </Stats>
</Filter>
