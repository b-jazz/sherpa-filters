Spam Through Foreign Mail Servers

The Internet is very much a global entity these days, and for some reason, spammers like to hid out in countries far away from US laws on spam (not that any good laws really exist.) Whenever I see an email that goes through a foreign mail server, I consider it spam. The method works pretty good for me so far. I have a small list of top level domains that I allow through and the rest go into the bit bucket. To do this, you can use the RelayDomain criteria demonstrated below.

Example

<Filter Name="SPAM: Foreign Mail Servers" Enabled="true" Tag="Spam">
  <Criteria>
    <RelayDomain>
      <Good>.com</Good>
      <Good>.net</Good>
      <Good>.edu</Good>
      <Good>.gov</Good>
      <Good>.org</Good>
      <Good>.us</Good>
      <Good>.uk</Good>
      <Good>.pl</Good>
    </RelayDomain>
  </Criteria>
  <Actions>
    <Move>
      <Destination>Mailboxes/Spam</Destination>
    </Move>
  </Actions>
  <Stats>
    <TestCounter>0</TestCounter>
    <MatchCounter>0</MatchCounter>
    <FilterTime>0</FilterTime>
    <LastMatchTime></LastMatchTime>
  </Stats>
</Filter>
