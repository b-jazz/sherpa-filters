ADV In The Subject Line

Some government agency decided that the best way to stop spam was to make it a law that you had to put "ADV:" as the first four letters of the subject line to make it clear to the receiver that they were receiving an advertisement. On one hand, this didn't stop spam, but on the other hand, some spammers are following the rule. Although some are trying to get cute by putting ADV somewhere else in the subject line instead of the start.

Examples

Here are three examples of how to write the subject line value to look for, depending on how strict you want to be.

  1. Only look for ADV at the start of the subject line.
    <Value>^ADV</Value>
  2. Look for ADV surrounded by non-word characters like (ADV), [ADV], and the like. Note that we want zero or one non-word characters at the start since we still want to catch ADV at the start of the line.
    <Value>\W?ADV\W+</Value>
  3. Look for ADV anywhere in the subject line. Be careful though, this might catch words like advice. The current version of Sherpa forces you to use case insensitive searches, but that will change in the next minor release.
    <Value>ADV</Value>

Complete Filter Example

<Filter Name="SPAM: ADV In Subject Line" Enabled="true" Tag="Spam">
  <Criteria>
    <HeaderMatch>
      <Header>Subject</Header>
      <Value>\W?ADV\W+</Value>
    </HeaderMatch>
  </Criteria>
  <Actions>
    <Move>
      <Destination>Mailboxes/Spam</Destination>
    </Move>
  </Actions>
  <Stats>
    <TestCounter>0</TestCounter>
    <MatchCounter>0</MatchCounter>
    <FilterTime>0</FilterTime>
    <LastMatchTime></LastMatchTime>
  </Stats>
</Filter>
